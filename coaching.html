What is Ashtanga Yoga?

Ashtanga name comes from sanskrit language from ancient India. Ashta meaning eight and Anga means limbs or components. Hence, Ashtanga can be translated to eight limbs of yoga as described by Patanjali in his yoga sutras. Patanjali is the foremost sages who taught knowledge of yoga to humanity from much older traditions. The 8 eight limbs are as follows: 1. Yamas (abstinences) 2. Niyamas (observensas) 3. Asana (postures) 4. Pranayama (breathing) 5. Pratyahara (withdrawal) 6. Dharana (concentration) 7. Dhyana (meditation) 8. Samadhi (Adsorption) Each of the above components have much more to be explained in detail, it requires interest and dedication from a student to learn more!

What happens in a session?

Most of Seven’s sessions are currently held online via Zoom. To participate in such a session with Seven, you first require a laptop or phone with a microphone and webcam; as well as a yoga mat. Seven typically teaches Ashtanga Vinyasa yoga, whch is one of the most popular yoga Asana practices in the present time for its benefits. It’s a dynamic yoga asana practice, synchronizing breath and movement in a flowing sequence of postures. The sequence becomes familiar and develops a self practice. It becomes ones own personal experience of movement and breath awareness! That being said, each session with Seven is different since it is tailored to given individual, according to their present ability.
